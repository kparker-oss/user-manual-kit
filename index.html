How I communicate

I'm async-first by default — most things can be handled over Slack. I respond quickly and I close loops. If you DM me something that needs an answer, I will get back to you. I use threads intentionally: when I ask you to reply in thread, I mean it. It keeps things findable.

When something is complex, I go long — detailed bullet points, context, clear owners, explicit deadlines. When something is simple, I go short. I calibrate based on what the situation actually needs, not out of habit. If you get a two-line message from me, it means the situation is clear. If you get five bullets with a cc list, something important is happening.

I will ping you more than once if I need something. I'm not impatient — I just refuse to let things fall through on my watch. "Me again!" is not an apology; it's a flag that something still needs resolution.

How to work with me

What works
  • Give me full context upfront — the who, what, when, and why
  • Be specific about deadlines and who owns what
  • Reply in threads when I ask for it
  • Show up when you say you will — especially for community events
  • Let me know when something changes; I'm tracking a lot
  • Bring me your ideas early — I can help shape and build on them
What slows me down
  • Assigning tasks without a deadline or a named owner
  • Looping me in after a decision is already made
  • Slow-walking action items that are blocking other people
  • "Can we chat?" with no context attached
  • Bureaucratic layers blocking quick, obvious actions
  • Asking me to act on things in channels I'm not part of
